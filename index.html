What's broken
Six gaps in how bol's price rating works today
The 1–5 star rating controls which partners get the buy box, who can advertise, and who shows up in search. Partners have almost no visibility into how it works or when it changes.
01
No way to predict your rating
  • The thresholds for each star tier aren't published
  • You can't model what happens before changing a price
  • A competitor dropping their price can silently downgrade you
Most partners don't know what drives their rating · To confirm via survey
02
You find out too late
  • Rating drops show up as declining sales, not alerts
  • Buy box and ad spend are already lost by the time you notice
  • The portal only shows your current rating — no history
Estimated days-long gap between a drop and a response · To measure via platform logs
03
Same rules for every category
  • A €10 gap matters very differently on a €15 book vs. a €500 TV
  • Electronics buyers compare prices carefully; book buyers mostly don't
  • Identical bands produce unfair outcomes across categories
Price sensitivity varies significantly by category · Source: ScienceDirect 2024
04
Rating ignores your margin
  • The system rewards the lowest price, regardless of profitability
  • Partners sometimes lower prices below cost to gain a star
  • There's no warning before you make that mistake
A meaningful share of rating-driven adjustments reduce net margin · To quantify with cost data
05
Automated pricing creates instability
  • 40%+ of top listings have multiple automated re-pricers active
  • Two algorithms competing can inflate prices or crash them
  • Neither outcome is good — and nothing flags it today
Validated · Source: ScienceDirect 2024 (bol.com dataset)
06
Partners pay third parties to fill the gap
  • The portal has no simulation, history, or prediction tools
  • Partners use Channable, Omnia, PriceTweakers instead
  • €3M+ a year leaving the bol ecosystem for tools bol should own
Estimated from tool pricing × active partner base
The other side
Why bol hasn't just made this transparent
There are real reasons the system works the way it does. Fixing it isn't just a question of building tools — it involves tradeoffs bol has to consciously make.
Arguments for more transparency
  • Partners who understand the system price more competitively
  • Fewer errors means fewer support tickets and disputes
  • Bringing tools in-house keeps that spend on-platform
  • Healthy partner economics = longer-term platform commitment
Arguments for keeping it opaque
  • Exact thresholds get gamed — partners price to the line, not below it
  • Ambiguity gives bol flexibility to adjust the system without backlash
  • Margin floors could reduce price pressure that benefits consumers
  • Category-level calibration is expensive to build and maintain
The middle ground: Show partners where they stand and what to change — without publishing the exact formula. Partners get clarity; bol keeps control.

Biggest structural change
Pricing bands should differ by category
Today every category uses the same deviation bands. These are indicative proposals — the right numbers come from bol's own conversion data, not assumptions.
Proposed threshold changes
The "Why" column draws on published retail behaviour research and category intuition — not bol's internal data. Actual thresholds need bol's conversion data to confirm.
Category How price-sensitive are buyers? Current 4★ threshold Proposed threshold Why
Consumer electronics Very ≤5% ≤3% 5% on a €500 TV is €25 — buyers notice and compare
Toys & games Moderate ≤5% ≤7% Brand and availability matter more than a €1–2 difference
Books & media Low ≤5% ≤12% Fixed cover prices and low absolute values; buyers rarely compare
Personal care Moderate ≤5% ≤6% Brand loyalty is high; repeat buyers are less price-driven
Fashion & footwear Moderate ≤5% ≤8% Size availability and style win over small price gaps
Source basis: Price sensitivity direction informed by ScienceDirect 2024 (bol.com dataset) and established retail consumer behaviour research. Specific thresholds are illustrative — calibration requires measuring where click-through rate drops materially vs. the buy box winner, by category, using bol's internal data.
